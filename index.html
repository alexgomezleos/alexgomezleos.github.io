Publications

  • Simple and Nearly-Optimal Sampling for Rank-1 Tensor Completion via Gauss-Jordan
    with Oscar Lopez
    Pre-print.
    Summary: We reduce rank-1 tensor completion to solving a special pair of random linear systems. This view allows us to bound the number of required samples for the original problem. The sample complexity of the latter is given by a matrix sketching problem over GF2, which can't be analyzed using leverage score sampling. To circumvent this, we study a random walk mixing time on a subspace graph.

  • Single-Server Private Information Retrieval With Side Information Under Arbitrary Popularity Profiles
    with Anoosheh Heidarzadeh
    Information Theory Workshop (ITW), 2022. (conference version) (slides)
    Summary: Suppose you want to obtain some item from a database without revealing its identity. A common two-party protocol based on linear codes can ensure perfect privacy guarantees. In the prior work, these guarantees break down if the items are not equally likely to be queried. We developed a randomized protocol to solve the problem. A key technical ingredient was proving an extremal property of our codes.

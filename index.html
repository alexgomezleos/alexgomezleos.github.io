I am a third-year PhD student in the Wireless Networking and Communications Group at UT Austin, advised by Sanjay Shakkottai. While at UT, my studies have been generously supported by the Virginia & Ernest Cockrell, Jr. Fellowship in Engineering. Before that, I earned a BS in Electrical and Computer Engineering from Texas A&M University, working with Anoosheh Heidarzadeh.

I enjoy topics in probability, combinatorics, and optimization---often in the context of randomized algorithms. My most immediate interest is in understanding the statistical and computational hardness of fundamental problems in modern data science. I am also broadly interested in theoretical explanations for empirical phenomena in experimental ML.

For these reasons, I gravitate towards these buzzwords: Bandits/RL, self-supervised learning, distributional testing, robust statistics, convex optimization. Please get in touch if we share interests!

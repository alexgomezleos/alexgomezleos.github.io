I am a Masters student in the Wireless Networking and Communications Group at UT Austin, advised by Sanjay Shakkottai. While at UT, my studies have been generously supported by the Virginia & Ernest Cockrell, Jr. Fellowship in Engineering. Before that, I earned a BS in Electrical and Computer Engineering from Texas A&M University, working with Anoosheh Heidarzadeh.

I enjoy studying complexity bounds for algorithmic problems. Lately, I've been interested in lower bounds for fundamental problems under memory or communication constraints. One of my favorite examples is the time-space tradeoff for parity learning. I think its facsinating that often the "textbook" algorithm for a task fails once stripped of resources we usually take for granted, like memory. Other than lower bounds for resource constrained problems, my interest is also caught in sublinear algorithms (i.e. when can we test a property faster than we can learn it?), as well as mechanism design (i.e. how to deal with strategic agents?) Topics that I've spent time more time thinking about includes algorithms for multi-armed bandits, MDPs, distribution testing, and more recently, low-rank tensor estimation.
